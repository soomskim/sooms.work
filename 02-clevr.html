CLEVR

2018, MIT Game Lab x The Education Arcade

UI/UX, tablet interface design, icons

The Collaborative Learning Experiences in Virtual Reality ⚛︎CLEVR⚛︎ is an interactive educational two-player game that seeks to explore how cross/extended reality (XR) and game-based approaches can aid in education; specifically in a highschool student's understanding of a cell.

The game is played with two players - an explorer (VR) and a navigator (tablet). The Explorer is virtually placed inside a 3D rendered cell, and their partner, the Navigator, interacts with an informative tablet screen to help guide them.

Advisors: Philip Tan, Dan Roy
Team: Darius Bopp, Therese Mills, Katharina Gschwind, Temi Taylor, Femi Oladipupo, Valeria Yang

As the sole UI Designer on the team, I was tasked with updating the tablet interface. The challenge was to present all information and features without overwhelming the user.

I designed more cohesive icons for pre-existing buttons and reaarranged the layout following results from user testings.

Ongoing research findings and biweekly user testings would inform new functions in the game, resulting in regular revitalizations of buttons and their behaviors.
